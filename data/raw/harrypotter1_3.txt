[Overview]
Harry Potter is a series of seven fantasy novels written by British author J. K. Rowling. The novels chronicle the lives of a young wizard, Harry Potter, and his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, who are students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

The central narrative follows Harry's conflict with Lord Voldemort, a dark wizard seeking immortality and domination over both the magical world and non-magical people, known as Muggles. Voldemort's goal includes overthrowing the Ministry of Magic, the governing body of the wizarding world.

[Themes and Genres]
The Harry Potter series blends multiple genres, including fantasy, drama, coming-of-age fiction, and the British school story. It also incorporates elements of mystery, thriller, adventure, horror, and romance.

Major themes explored throughout the series include:
- Prejudice
- Corruption
- Madness
- Love
- Death

[Reception and Impact]
Since the publication of the first novel in 1997, the Harry Potter books have achieved immense global popularity and commercial success. They have attracted a wide readership across both children and adults and are often regarded as foundational works of modern popular literature.

As of February 2023, the series has sold more than 600 million copies worldwide, making it the best-selling book series in history. The final four installments each broke records as the fastest-selling books of their time. The final book sold approximately 2.7 million copies in the United Kingdom and 8.3 million copies in the United States within 24 hours of release.

The series holds the Guinness World Record for the best-selling book series for children.

[Film Adaptations and Franchise]
Warner Bros. Pictures adapted the seven novels into an eight-part film series released between 2001 and 2011. In 2016, the total value of the Harry Potter franchise was estimated at approximately 25 billion USD, making it one of the highest-grossing media franchises of all time.

Additional expansions of the franchise include:
- The stage play *Harry Potter and the Cursed Child*
- A television series adaptation currently in production at HBO
- A spin-off film trilogy beginning with *Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them* (2016)

[Expanded Universe]
The success of the books and films led to numerous derivative works, including:
- A travelling exhibition first launched in 2009
- The Warner Bros. Studio Tour London (opened in 2012)
- A digital platform providing expanded lore and commentary by J. K. Rowling
- Theme park attractions collectively known as *The Wizarding World of Harry Potter*, located at Universal Destinations & Experiences parks worldwide
